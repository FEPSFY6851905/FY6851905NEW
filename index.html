The Evolution of Surgery

1. Surgical Techniques

There are three approaches to surgery:

  • Open Surgery: Large incision to access organs, used for centuries.
  • Laparoscopic Surgery: Small cuts with instruments and a camera, developed in the 1970s-1980s.
  • Robotic Surgery: Surgeons operate using a controller, enhancing precision and flexibility, introduced around 2000.

2. Growth of Robotic Surgery

Robotic surgery's adoption has grown significantly:

  • In the US, it increased from ~2% of surgeries in 2012 to ~18% by 2018.
  • Robotic techniques often replace open and laparoscopic methods.

Reference: JAMA Network Study

3. Variations in Adoption

Robotic surgery varies by type:

  • More common in simpler surgeries (e.g., hernia repair: 22-28% in 2018).
  • Slower adoption in complex procedures (e.g., cancer resections: 2.1% in 2012 to 3.9% in 2018).

4. Advantages and Disadvantages

Advantages

  • Reduced surgeon fatigue and burnout.
  • Lower patient complications like blood clots.

Read more: Wiley Online Library

Disadvantages

  • Longer operating times increase costs.
  • Lack of tactile feedback for surgeons.
  • Robotic faults occur in 0.4%-4.6% of cases.
